# VL-veluweFLM
Forests are among the most biodiverse ecosystems that once covered large parts of
Europe. However, with increasing land use intensity, forest cover declined as well as
biodiversity in the remaining forests. To better understand forest biodiversity dynamics
and to halt biodiversity loss, we need an integrated approach that enables
understanding and prediction of the current and future impacts of direct and indirect
anthropogenic drivers on forest biodiversity.

The Veluwe forest ecosystem is one of the Netherlands' largest contiguous forest areas
and a designated Natura 2000 area that is heavily affected by nitrogen deposition. At
the level of the Veluwe, impacts of global change, national policy and local management
come together.

With this goal and scope in mind the LTER-LIFE Veluwe team is
developing a forest landscape model of the Veluwe. Since February 2025 it serves as a
LTER-LIFE Veluwe use case.
